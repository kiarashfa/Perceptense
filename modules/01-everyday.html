⚖️ Mass vs. Weight — what's the difference?

In everyday speech, "weight" and "mass" are used interchangeably — and that's fine. But technically: mass is the amount of matter in an object (measured in kg), while weight is the force gravity exerts on that mass (measured in newtons). Your mass is the same on the Moon as on Earth; your weight is about 6× less on the Moon because gravity is weaker there. When you stand on a bathroom scale, it's technically measuring force and displaying it as kg — a convenient convention. In this module and in daily life, "weight" and "mass" mean the same thing.

Your weight across the solar system

Values shown as equivalent kg on a bathroom scale (force ÷ 9.807 m/s²). Your mass never changes — only the gravitational pull does.

Rules of thumb worth memorising
×2.2
To convert kg → pounds, multiply by 2.2. (1 kg = 2.205 lb)
÷28
Grams to ounces: divide by 28. (28.35 g = 1 oz)
×1000
1 tonne = 1,000 kg = 1 metric ton. A car ≈ 1.4 tonnes.
density
Water = 1 g/ml. Iron = 7.9. Gold = 19.3. Wood floats because it's <1 g/ml.
stones
UK/Irish body weight: 1 stone = 14 pounds = 6.35 kg. A 70 kg person = 11 stone.
Part G · test yourself

1. A bag of flour at the supermarket is typically how heavy?

1 kg. Sometimes also sold in 500g or 2kg bags, but 1kg is the standard. Anchor: same as 1 litre of water.

2. How much does a full 5-person family (2 adults + 3 children) weigh together, roughly?

Roughly 250–310 kg. Two adults ~70 kg each = 140 kg. Three children (mixed ages, including young ones) can range widely — perhaps 20–50 kg each. A rough middle estimate gives ~110–170 kg for the children, making the family total around 250–310 kg. Less than a quarter of a car's weight.

3. Someone says "this package weighs about 2 pounds." What is that in kg?

About 0.9 kg — essentially 1 kg. The rule to remember: 1 pound ≈ 0.45 kg, so 2 pounds ≈ 0.9 kg. For rough estimates, "2 pounds ≈ 1 kg" is close enough.

4. A gold bar (the kind held in bank vaults) — how heavy is it?

The standard "Good Delivery" bar used by central banks weighs 400 troy ounces — about 12.4 kg. That's roughly the weight of a full carry-on suitcase. Gold is extremely dense: the bar is about the size of a large smartphone, yet you cannot lift it with one hand comfortably.

5. You're mailing a letter. The envelope + single page inside weighs roughly how much?

About 8–12 g total. A standard envelope is ~5–6 g; one A4 page folded inside is ~5 g. This is why postal services often set the cheapest letter rate at "up to 20g" — it comfortably covers a single-sheet letter with room to spare.

6. Which is heavier: a litre of olive oil, or a litre of water?

Water is heavier. Olive oil has a density of ~0.91 g/ml, so a litre weighs about 910 g versus exactly 1,000 g for water. This is why oil floats on water — it's less dense. A litre of honey, by contrast, weighs ~1,400 g — 40% heavier than water.
